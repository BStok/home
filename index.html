# self-hosted deployment

Paste a repo.
Get a live URL.

Parsley clones your GitHub repository, figures out what it is, builds a container, and runs it behind HTTPS — without you writing a Dockerfile or touching a server.

how it works
$parsley deploy https://github.com/you/your-project
├─clone pulls your repo from the URL you give it — public repos, no setup
├─detect reads your files to figure out the framework, port, and start command
├─build generates the right Dockerfile and builds a container image
├─deploy runs your container and routes traffic to it through HTTPS
└─watch push to main and Parsley rebuilds and redeploys automatically
